## Abstract
Partially Observable Markov Decision Processes (POMDPs) provide a framework for modeling decision-making under uncertain information observed from the interaction environment. However, solving them is computationally challenging due to the need to maintain probability distributions in a potentially exponentially large state space.

This Master's thesis, completed under the supervision of **Prof. Mihai Anitescu** at the University of Chicago, focuses on online planning algorithms to address these challenges. The research primarily involves:
* Studying the application of the Partially Observable Monte Carlo Planning (POMCP) algorithm with Upper Confidence bounds for Trees (UCT).
* Formally converting the POMDP into a history-based MDP, which provides the theoretical foundation for proving the algorithm's convergence.
* Proving the convergence of the POMCP algorithm.
* Implementing and validating the algorithm on the classical high-dimensional RockSample problem.
