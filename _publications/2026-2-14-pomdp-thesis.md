## Abstract
[cite_start]Partially Observable Markov Decision Processes (POMDPs) provide a framework for modeling decision-making under uncertain information observed from the interaction environment[cite: 88]. [cite_start]However, solving them is computationally challenging due to the need to maintain probability distributions in a potentially exponentially large state space[cite: 89].

This Master's thesis, completed under the supervision of **Prof. [cite_start]Mihai Anitescu** at the University of Chicago[cite: 85], focuses on online planning algorithms to address these challenges. The research primarily involves:
* Studying the application of the Partially Observable Monte Carlo Planning (POMCP) algorithm with Upper Confidence bounds for Trees (UCT).
* [cite_start]Formally converting the POMDP into a history-based MDP, which provides the theoretical foundation for proving the algorithm's convergence[cite: 121].
* [cite_start]Proving the convergence of the POMCP algorithm.
* Implementing and validating the algorithm on the classical high-dimensional RockSample problem[cite: 90, 122].
